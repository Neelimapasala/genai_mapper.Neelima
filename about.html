Our Mission

Why We Exist
Mission

We believe powerful natural language processing should not require a machine learning background or a dedicated data-science team. GenAI Knowledge Mapper wraps state-of-the-art models behind a clean, intuitive interface — so anyone from a PhD student to a business analyst can extract entities, visualize parse trees, and build citation networks in seconds.

Six-Stage NLP Pipeline

How It Works

Every document follows a precise processing sequence — from raw input to interactive, exportable insights.

STEP 01
Text Ingestion

Raw text or uploaded documents (TXT, DOCX, PDF, CSV) are ingested, validated, and queued for the processing pipeline.

Input Layer
STEP 02
Normalization

Whitespace collapsed, special characters filtered, text truncated to optimal processing length with smart boundary detection.

Preprocessing
STEP 03
NER + POS Tagging

spaCy identifies 18+ entity types and assigns Penn Treebank POS tags to every token for downstream analysis.

spaCy
STEP 04
SVO Relation Extraction

Subject-Verb-Object triplets extracted via dependency parsing, revealing semantic relationships between entities.

Dependency Parse
STEP 05
LLaMA AI Summary

Groq LLaMA-3.3-70B generates a concise 3–5 sentence research-grade summary with key insights and themes.

Groq LLaMA 70B
STEP 06
RAG-Powered QA

Chunks indexed into a retrieval engine. Ask any question about your document — answers are grounded in your actual content, not hallucinated.

RAG Engine

Meet the Founder

The Team
Founder
"I built GenAI Mapper because
researchers deserve better tools."

Most NLP platforms are built for engineers. Neelima saw PhD students and domain experts struggling with command-line tools, opaque model outputs, and disconnected workflows. She built this platform to close that gap — making constituency parse trees, entity extraction, citation mapping, and RAG-powered document QA as simple as pasting a paragraph of text.

Every design decision — from the glassmorphism dark theme to the one-click CSV exports — is grounded in what researchers actually need during a literature review, not what makes a nice demo.

N
Neelima Pasala
Founder & Full-Stack Developer

Passionate about the intersection of generative AI and natural language processing, Neelima designed every layer of this platform — from the spaCy extraction pipeline to the Groq-powered parse tree renderer — with clarity and accessibility as the primary goals. Based in Chennai, India.
